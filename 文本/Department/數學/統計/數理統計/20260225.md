# Example：
$$
\begin{align}
&x_1,\ldots,x_n\overset{\text{i.i.d.}}{\sim}\mathrm{Poisson}(\theta)\\
&\text{Let } \tau(\theta)=e^{-\theta}
\end{align}
$$
(1). Find and unbiased estimator of $\tau(\theta)$
(2). Obtain an unbiased estimator of $\tau(\theta)$ whose variance is smaller than taht found in (1)

# Sol:
(1)
$$
\begin{alignat}{2}
&f(x;\theta)&=\frac{\theta^xe^{-\theta}}{x!}\quad&x=0,1,2,3\ldots
\end{alignat}
$$
> [!info] Note that
> In Poisson distribution $e^{-\theta}$ is probability
> $P(X=0)=\frac{\theta^0e^{-\theta}}{0!}$

$$
\begin{align}
\text{Let }U&=I_{\left\lbrace0\right\rbrace}(x_1)=
\begin{cases}
1,&x_1=0\\
0,&\text{o.w}
\end{cases}\\\\
E(U)&=1\cdot P(U=1)+0\cdot P(U-0)\\
&=P(U=1)\\
&=P(x_1=0)\\
&=e^{-\theta}
\end{align}
$$
Hence, U is an unbiased estimator of $\tau(\theta)$.

(2)
1. unbiased $U=I_{\left\lbrace0\right\rbrace}(x_1)$
2. sufficient $T=\sum^n_{i=1}X_i$
3. 
$$
\begin{align}
f_{U\mid T}(1\mid t)&=\frac{P(U=1,T=t)}{P(T=t)}\\\\
&=\frac{P(X_1=0,\sum^{n}_{i=1}X_i=t)}{P(\sum^{n}_{i=1}X_i=t)}\\\\
&=\frac{P(X=0,\sum^{n}_{i=2}X_i=t)}{P(\sum^{n}_{i=1}X_i=t)}\\\\
&=\frac{P(X_1=0)\cdot P(\sum^{n}_{i=2}X_i=t)}{P(\sum^{n}_{i=1}X_i=t)}\\\\
&=\frac{e^{-\theta}\frac{((n-1)\theta)^t e^{-(n-1)\theta}}{t!}}{\frac{(n\theta)^te^{-n\theta}}{t!}}\\
&=\left(1-\frac1n\right)^t
\end{align}
$$
